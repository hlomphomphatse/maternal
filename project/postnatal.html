Postnatal Care

What is Postnatal Care ?

Postnatal Care is the care given to a woman and her baby after the baby is born. This special care lasts for about 6 weeks, starting right after the baby is born and continuing until most of the mother's body has returned to the way it was before she became pregnant. It's a time when the mother and baby need extra attention to ensure they both stay healthy.

Breastfeeding Support

Breastfeeding support encompasses guidance, education,and emotional assistance provided to new mothers to ensure successful and comfortable breastfeeding experiences.
Breastfeeding support is crucial because it has many health benefits for both mothers and babies. It helps keep them healthy. It also ensures that everyone, no matter where they come from, gets the same access to good healthcare. It's there to help moms feel happy and provides solutions when things get tough. It gives moms the confidence to make choices, and it's like being part of a supportive community. It can save money, and it makes it easier for moms to continue working if they want to.

Mental Health

Mental health support is a vital component of postnatal care, focusing on the emotional well-being of new mothers during the postpartum period. It addresses the full spectrum of emotions that new mothers experience, from joy to anxiety and stress, aiming to help them cope with these feelings and maintain emotional well-being
This support is essential in identifying, managing, and seeking treatment for postpartum depression and anxiety, which are common during this time. Additionally, it plays a pivotal role in fostering strong bonds with the baby, maintaining healthy family relationships, and supporting effective parenting.
Recognizing that a mother's mental health can impact her physical well-being, it contributes to overall health during the postnatal period. By encouraging mothers to seek help when needed, it creates a supportive environment for addressing concerns and has long-term positive impacts on both the mother and the child's development.
